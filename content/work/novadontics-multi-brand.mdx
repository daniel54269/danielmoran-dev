## The role

Novadontics is a dental software / clinical-services group that runs three customer-facing brands: **Novadontics** (the software), **Care Pulse** (the operational arm), and **California Implant Institute** (the training and events arm). I came in as **Director of Marketing reporting to the CEO**, with all three brand budgets, all three websites, and all three marketing tracking stacks consolidated under one P&L.

## What was broken

- **Three different HubSpot lifecycle stages** in three different shapes. A "Lead" in Novadontics meant something completely different from a "Lead" in Care Pulse. The CRO couldn't answer "how many leads did we have last week" without a phone call.
- **Three different GTM containers**, three different attribution definitions. Spend on one brand was double-counted against the others' pipeline.
- **No weekly leadership review.** The CEO got slack updates when something shipped, not a recurring number-driven check-in.

## The 90 days

**Days 1–14: discovery + thesis.** Map every channel, every spend dollar, every lifecycle stage across all three brands. Identify the single thing that, if fixed, unblocks the rest.

The bottleneck: lifecycle + attribution. Couldn't make budget decisions without it.

**Days 15–60: rebuild.**

- One unified HubSpot lifecycle model — the same stages mean the same things across all three brands.
- One GTM container per brand but with shared, namespaced events. One reporting view across all three.
- Make.com automations replacing three different manual lead-routing flows. Each brand keeps its handoff rules; ops time drops to near zero.
- The Director (me) plus 1 part-time analyst replaces what was a fragmented set of agency engagements.

**Days 61–90: operating cadence.**

- Weekly leadership review with the CEO. One slide. Top line: pipeline by brand. Below: spend efficiency, content velocity, top three risks.
- Owned the events motion for California Implant Institute — represented the brand at industry events, brought direct pipeline conversations into the CRM the same week.
- Quarterly target-setting in the new shared HubSpot stages, so the CEO and I were arguing about the right things.

## Outcome

- **One source of truth** for marketing across three brands and one CEO who could answer "how are we doing" without a phone call.
- **3 → 1 tracking system** consolidation; 6 hours/week of manual reporting recovered.
- **Closed-loop attribution** from first touch to clinical / software pipeline stages, by brand.
- **A reporting cadence** the CEO trusted enough to use in board updates.

## Why this is the leadership story

I treat marketing like a product team. The job isn't "more posts." The job is: pick the bottleneck, ship the rebuild, install a cadence the executive team will actually use. Novadontics is the case study where that was the explicit ask.

The same playbook is what I'd run as Head of Marketing at an early-stage B2B SaaS. The shape of the company changes; the order of operations doesn't.
